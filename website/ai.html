Using AI in Stratford-upon-Avon

The new generation of AI tools lets small teams do work that used to need ten people.

You don't need to be a research lab to benefit. Most of the leverage is in mundane things: drafting, summarising, looking through documents, automating the bits of admin that drain a week. We help local founders and businesses figure out where to start.

Where AI actually helps

The hype cycle makes it hard to tell signal from noise. The honest answer is that AI is genuinely good at a narrow set of things, and pretending otherwise wastes money. The areas where small businesses tend to get real value:

  • Writing first drafts — proposals, emails, listings, internal docs.
  • Reading through long documents and pulling out what matters.
  • Glueing together small bits of automation that don't justify a full IT project.
  • Customer-facing assistants for FAQ-style questions.
  • Coding help for non-developers building internal tools.

Who this is for

Small businesses in and around Stratford who suspect AI might help but don't have the time to evaluate the field. Founders shipping products who want to move faster. People who want to understand what's possible before they spend on a tool.

How we work

Informally. A conversation, a look at what you're actually doing day-to-day, and a recommendation that's grounded in your situation rather than what's trending on Twitter. Often the answer is a free tool used well; sometimes it's a small custom build.
